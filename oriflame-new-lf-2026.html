CREATIVE DIRECTION

Core art direction + design principles for more premium / luxury beauty and fashion brands.

Colour

Almost all luxury brands drastically reduce their colour palette to a more minimal range across design elements and page furniture. Often the primary colours are just Black, White, Grey and one/two lightly used Brand Colours.

This allows colour to be provided by the products and the main focus to be on them, not the brand.

Photography

We can improve the overall design of the site by focussing on the art direction of both model and product photography.

Models (Smiling)

Most luxury brands don't use people smiling. It somehow makes brands look 'cheaper' or more 'lower-end'.

Model POV (eyes)

Rather counter-intuitively it's not encouraged for models to be looking directly to camera when designing for luxury. They should if possible be looking slightly off-camera, or if they are looking towards camera it should be from an angle, or a side view.

There is significant empirical research, in fashion photography specifically, that suggests there can be up to a 20% increase in user clickthroughs with models that are looking '1) away from camera', compared to '2) looking directly to camera'. This has been attributed to 2) being more aspirational, therefore more enticing.

Try to find or use images that follow these rules and it will increase the overall premium quality of design.

Cropping

Close cropping is always encouraged on models and also product art.

Sizing

Within product listings and product cards, the product imagery is too closely sized and scaled. We should try and allow more 'air' around product cards when grouped together so they stand out from each other.

Typography + Copy

We can improve the overall premium design of the site by creating better type hierarchy and also creating better, and more importantly shorter, copy headlines.

Create better type hierarchy

A better use of the Bold and Regular versions of the Oriflame 3.0 fonts. We would advise avoiding using the Bold version unless it is within copy, or used to highlight text. The Regular (or lighter) of the font is more elegant. There is a widespread use of CAPS and lowercase throughout – it needs to be standardised. And there are lots of inconsistencies of the Type Kerning and Spacing.

Create better and shorter copy headlines

We can improve headlines and design in general by shortening and rearranging focus of the copy.

Page Grids, Layouts + Components

We can improve the overall premium design feel of the site by adjusting the page grid structure, and increasing spacing and sizing throughout.

Motion + micro-interactions

We can further improve the overall premium design feel of the site by adding subtle motion in page scrolling and micro-interactions across components and user engagement.

This all adds to the luxury feel of the brand and experience.

Page scroll parallax effects

A simple thing we can add is section parallax effects - fade up on scroll appear - is one of the simplest and most effective techniques used by luxury brands.

Adding micro-interactions and ‘moments of delight’ for users during interactions can help to enhance the experience.
